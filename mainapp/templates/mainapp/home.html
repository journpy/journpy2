Constructionism

In the 1980â€™s, Seymour Papert introduced the term constructionism as a learning model based on the theory of constructivism. Constructionism is a learning theory in which students learn by creating shareable artefacts.

It advocates that students can learn better by manipulating objects and creating artefacts at different phases of learning. Students learn in a practical and hands-on manner as opposed to the traditional method of teaching. According to Papert, the primary tool used in constructionist learning is the computer.

The computer is the object that learners use to think with as they create personal digital artefacts that represents their own learning. Students enjoy constructionist learning because it gives them ownership of their learning as they use technology to create their own learning experiences. Constructionism promotes time management in learning. It also allows students to learn from their mistakes which is similar to the concept of debugging - the act of identifying and correcting errors in a computer program.
